# Base Paper Analysis

## Project Title

**Adaptive Low-Power Congestion-Aware NoC Router for FPGA**

## Base Paper Selection Criteria

The base paper should be:

- Closely related to NoC router design.
- Relevant to adaptive routing or congestion management.
- Relevant to low-power NoC design where possible.
- Technically suitable for extending into an FPGA-oriented implementation.
- Published in a recognized IEEE journal.
- Preferably published between 2025 and 2026.

## Candidate Journal Papers from Literature Survey

### Candidate 1

**Title:** BuffeRS: A Buffer Reservation Scheduling Strategy for Router Bypassing in NoCs and Multichiplet Networks

**Publication:** IEEE Transactions on Computer-Aided Design of Integrated Circuits and Systems (IEEE TCAD)

**Year:** 2026

**Relevance:**

This paper is highly relevant to router architecture, buffer management, and improving NoC communication performance through router bypassing and buffer reservation.

**Potential Use as Base Paper:**

The concepts related to buffer utilization and router-level optimization may provide a foundation for developing an FPGA-oriented router architecture.

## Proposed Project

**Adaptive Low-Power Congestion-Aware NoC Router for FPGA**

## Planned Extension

The proposed work will focus on developing a practical FPGA-oriented NoC router that combines:

- Congestion monitoring
- Adaptive routing
- Low-power operation
- 5-port router architecture
- Verilog RTL implementation
- FPGA-based evaluation

The final technical differences between the base paper and proposed project will be documented after selecting the final base paper.
