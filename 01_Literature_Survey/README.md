# Literature Survey

This folder contains the literature survey conducted for the project:

**Adaptive Low-Power Congestion-Aware NoC Router for FPGA**

The literature survey focuses on recent developments in Network-on-Chip (NoC) architectures, adaptive routing, congestion-aware routing, low-power NoC design, FPGA-based NoC implementation, fault-tolerant routing, and router architectures.

The surveyed literature primarily covers publications from **2024 to 2026**, with emphasis on recent developments relevant to the proposed FPGA-based NoC router.

## Main Research Areas

The literature survey covers the following research areas:

1. NoC Router Architectures
2. Adaptive Routing
3. Congestion-Aware Routing
4. Low-Power NoC Design
5. FPGA-Based NoC Implementation
6. Fault-Tolerant Routing
7. Energy-Efficient NoC Design
8. NoC Reliability
9. NoC Topology
10. Router Optimization

## Literature Survey Period

**2024–2026**

## Purpose of the Survey

The purpose of the literature survey is to:

- Understand existing NoC router architectures.
- Study adaptive routing techniques.
- Analyze congestion-aware routing methods.
- Investigate low-power design techniques.
- Study FPGA-based NoC implementations.
- Identify limitations in existing NoC router designs.
- Identify a suitable research gap.
- Define the scope and contribution of the proposed project.

## Current Research Direction

Based on the literature studied, the proposed project focuses on developing an:

**Adaptive Low-Power Congestion-Aware NoC Router for FPGA**

The proposed design will investigate the integration of congestion monitoring, adaptive routing, and low-power techniques in an FPGA-oriented NoC router architecture.
